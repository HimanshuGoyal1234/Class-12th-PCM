SHORT SUMMARY

"Lost Spring" is a poignant account of child labor in India, focusing on two main stories. The first follows Saheb, a ragpicker from Dhaka who now lives in Seemapuri, Delhi. The second story is about Mukesh, who belongs to a family of bangle-makers in Firozabad. Both stories highlight how poverty and social traditions force children into labor, depriving them of education and a normal childhood. The author exposes the harsh realities of these children's lives while questioning society's indifference to their plight. The title "Lost Spring" symbolizes how these children lose the springtime of their lives - their childhood - to labor and poverty.

Characters

Saheb-e-Alam

A young ragpicker from Seemapuri who migrated from Dhaka. He dreams of going to school, wearing shoes, and playing tennis.

Mukesh

A boy from Firozabad's bangle-making community who dreams of becoming a motor mechanic despite family traditions.

Savita

A young girl in Firozabad who works making bangles, symbolizing the endless cycle of poverty and tradition.

Theme

Child Labor

The story exposes how poverty forces children into labor, depriving them of education and childhood.

Poverty Cycle

Highlights how generations remain trapped in poverty due to lack of education and social constraints.

Social Inequality

Exposes the gap between India's rich and poor, and society's indifference to child labor.

Important Questions

Why does Saheb migrate to Seemapuri?

Saheb's family left Dhaka due to frequent storms that destroyed their homes and fields, coming to Seemapuri in search of livelihood.

What is the significance of the title "Lost Spring"?

It symbolizes how poor children lose their childhood (springtime of life) to labor and poverty.

Why do children in Firozabad not go to school?

They are forced into bangle-making due to family traditions, poverty, and the vicious cycle of exploitation by middlemen and authorities.

What does Mukesh want to become?

Mukesh dreams of becoming a motor mechanic, breaking away from his family's traditional bangle-making occupation.

What does the author mean by "a vicious circle"?

She refers to the endless cycle of poverty, child labor, and exploitation that traps generations of poor families.

What is the significance of Saheb's name?

His full name "Saheb-e-Alam" means "lord of the universe," which contrasts ironically with his impoverished reality.
