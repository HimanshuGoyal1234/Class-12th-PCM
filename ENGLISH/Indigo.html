Flamingo (Prose) – "Indigo" by Louis Fischer

SHORT SUMMARY

"Indigo" by Louis Fischer narrates Mahatma Gandhi's efforts to help poor indigo farmers in Champaran, Bihar. These farmers were forced by British landlords to grow indigo on a portion of their land and sell it at unfair prices. Gandhi’s visit and leadership led to their freedom from this exploitative system, marking the first step towards India's independence movement.

Characters

Mahatma Gandhi

The leader who fought against the exploitation of Champaran's indigo farmers.

Rajkumar Shukla

A poor but determined farmer who invited Gandhi to Champaran.

British Landlords

The oppressive landlords who forced Indian farmers into unfair indigo contracts.

Theme

Exploitation of Farmers

The story highlights the economic injustice faced by Indian farmers under British rule.

Gandhi’s Leadership

Gandhi’s non-violent methods and determination helped bring justice to the farmers.

Freedom and Self-Reliance

The story reflects India's struggle for independence and self-sufficiency.

Important Questions

Why did Rajkumar Shukla approach Gandhi?

He wanted Gandhi to visit Champaran and help the indigo farmers fight against British exploitation.

What was the issue faced by Champaran farmers?

They were forced to grow indigo on 15% of their land and sell it at unfair prices to British landlords.

How did Gandhi help the farmers?

He conducted an investigation, mobilized public support, and negotiated a settlement that benefitted the farmers.

What was the significance of the Champaran movement?

It was Gandhi’s first major political victory in India and marked the beginning of his role in the independence movement.

Key Quotes

"The real relief for them is to be free from fear." - Mahatma Gandhi

"What I did was a very ordinary thing. I declared that the British could not order me about in my own country." - Gandhi

Literary Devices

Irony

The British, who claimed to bring justice, were exploiting poor farmers.

Symbolism

Indigo represents the suffering of Indian farmers under colonial rule.
