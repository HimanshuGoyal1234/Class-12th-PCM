Flamingo (Prose) – "The Last Lesson" by Alphonse Daudet

SHORT SUMMARY

The story is set in Alsace, France, during the Franco-Prussian War (1870-71), when Prussia defeated France and took over Alsace and Lorraine. The narrator, Franz, is a young schoolboy who dreads school and dislikes learning French. One day, he arrives late to school and notices an unusual silence. His teacher, M. Hamel, announces that this is their last French lesson because a new order has come from Berlin: only German will be taught in schools. Franz regrets not paying attention in class earlier. M. Hamel delivers an emotional speech about the importance of language and how it is the key to a nation's identity and freedom. The villagers, including old Hauser, attend the class as a mark of respect. The lesson ends with M. Hamel writing "Vive La France!" on the board, overwhelmed with emotion.

Characters

Franz

The young narrator who initially dislikes school but gains new appreciation for his language and teacher during the last lesson.

M. Hamel

The French teacher who has taught at the school for 40 years. He delivers an emotional farewell lesson about the importance of language.

The Villagers

Including old Hauser, the former mayor, and former postmaster who attend the last lesson to show respect for their language.

Theme

Importance of Language

The story emphasizes how language is tied to cultural identity and freedom. M. Hamel calls it "the key to prison".

Patriotism

The story shows love for one's country through appreciation of its language and resistance to cultural oppression.

Regret

Franz and the villagers regret not valuing their language and culture until it was being taken away.

Important Questions

Why was Franz afraid of going to school that morning?

Franz was afraid because he was late and hadn't learned his French participles, expecting scolding from M. Hamel.

What was unusual about the school that day?

The school was strangely quiet (no noise of desks, recitations). Villagers sat in the back, and M. Hamel wore his formal suit.

What did M. Hamel say about the French language?

He called French the "most beautiful language"—the key to their identity and freedom, urging them to guard it.

Why were the villagers sitting in the classroom?

They came to show respect for M. Hamel's last lesson and regret not valuing education earlier.

What was written on the bulletin board?

The order from Berlin: "Only German will be taught in schools of Alsace and Lorraine."

Significance of M. Hamel's words:

He meant that language preserves culture—even under oppression, it's a tool for resistance and unity.

Key Quotes

"We've all a great deal to reproach ourselves with."

"When a people are enslaved, as long as they hold fast to their language it is as if they had the key to their prison."

"The French language is the most beautiful language in the world... the clearest, the most logical."

"Vive La France!"

Literary Devices

Symbolism

The French language symbolizes cultural identity and freedom

Irony

Franz only appreciates his lessons when they're ending

Imagery

Vivid descriptions of the classroom and M. Hamel's appearance

Metaphor

Language as the "key to prison"
