Flamingo (Prose) – "The Tiger King" by Kalki

SHORT SUMMARY

"The Tiger King" is a satirical story about the arrogance of power. It follows the life of the king of Pratibandapuram, who is prophesied to be killed by a tiger. Determined to escape his fate, he starts killing tigers to prevent the prophecy from coming true. However, fate has its own way, and in an ironic twist, he meets his end due to a wooden tiger. The story critiques blind superstition and the misuse of power.

Theme

Fate vs. Free Will

The story highlights how fate ultimately prevails despite human efforts to change destiny.

Arrogance of Power

The king’s unchecked authority leads him to irrational decisions, proving his downfall.

Irony and Satire

The story humorously criticizes superstitions and the foolishness of rulers.

Important Questions

What was the prophecy about the Tiger King?

It was predicted that the king would die because of a tiger.

How did the king try to escape his fate?

By killing 100 tigers to nullify the prophecy.

What is the irony in the story?

Despite killing 99 tigers, he dies because of a wooden tiger’s sliver.

Key Quotes

"The astrologers’ prediction came true in a manner he had never imagined."

"The Maharaja was firm in his resolve. From that day onwards, he started hunting tigers with a vengeance."

"The hundredth tiger took its final revenge upon the Tiger King."

Literary Devices

Irony

The Tiger King kills 99 tigers but dies because of a wooden one.

Satire

The story mocks the foolishness of rulers and blind faith in prophecies.

Foreshadowing

The astrologer’s prediction sets the stage for the king’s fate.
