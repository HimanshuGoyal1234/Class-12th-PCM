Vistas (Prose) – "Should Wizard Hit Mommy?" by John Updike

SHORT SUMMARY

The story explores the themes of childhood, parental authority, and differing perspectives on reality. It follows a father, Jack, who tells his daughter, Jo, a bedtime story about Roger Skunk, a creature who smells bad and is shunned by others. The wizard helps Roger by giving him a pleasant smell, but his mother disapproves and forces him to revert to his original state. Jo, who represents innocence and imagination, wants a different ending, but Jack insists on the traditional resolution, highlighting the contrast between a child's worldview and an adult's perspective.

Theme

Parental Authority vs. Childhood Imagination

Jack enforces traditional values, while Jo desires a different ending, showing a clash between perspectives.

Innocence vs. Experience

Jo's innocence contrasts with Jack's belief in accepting reality as it is.

Social Acceptance vs. Individuality

Roger Skunk's struggle reflects society’s pressure to conform.

Important Questions

Why did Roger Skunk go to the wizard?

He wanted to change his bad smell so that other animals would play with him.

Why was Jo unhappy with the ending?

She felt Roger’s mother was wrong and wanted the wizard to hit her.

What does the story suggest about adult-child relationships?

It highlights how adults impose their views on children, sometimes ignoring their feelings.

Key Quotes

"Then she said, 'But the other little animals run away!'"

"But Daddy, then he said about the wizard, 'Make him smell very bad again.'"

"She was starting to fuss with her hands and look up with red eyes."

Literary Devices

Symbolism

Roger Skunk represents the struggle for social acceptance.

Irony

Jo expects a happy ending, but reality contradicts her expectations.

Foreshadowing

Jo's insistence on changing the ending hints at her growing independence.
