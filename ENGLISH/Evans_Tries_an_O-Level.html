Flamingo (Prose) – "Evans Tries an O-Level" by Colin Dexter

SHORT SUMMARY

"Evans Tries an O-Level" is a humorous and thrilling story about an intelligent prisoner named Evans, who plans an elaborate escape while appearing to take an O-Level German exam. The prison authorities take extreme precautions to prevent his escape, but Evans outsmarts them through meticulous planning and deception. The story highlights themes of intelligence, deception, and the limitations of authority.

Theme

Deception and Intelligence

Evans uses his intelligence and cunning to deceive the authorities and escape from prison.

Overconfidence of Authority

The prison officials, despite their strict precautions, underestimate Evans, leading to his successful escape.

Crime and Punishment

The story explores the idea that criminals can sometimes outwit the law, raising questions about justice.

Important Questions

How does Evans plan his escape?

Evans disguises himself as the invigilator and manipulates the prison authorities into believing he is still in his cell.

Why was the Governor confident Evans wouldn’t escape?

The Governor believed his strict security measures would be foolproof, but he underestimated Evans' cunning nature.

What role does the German exam play in Evans' escape?

The exam is a clever cover for his escape plan, allowing him to bring in an accomplice disguised as an invigilator.

Key Quotes

"He’s just a congenital kleptomaniac."

"A quiet sort of chap, but he’s got no record of violence."

"Evans the Break has escaped again!"

Literary Devices

Irony

The authorities take extra precautions, yet Evans manages to escape right under their noses.

Foreshadowing

Hints about Evans' cleverness and past escapes build suspense throughout the story.

Humor

The lighthearted tone and Evans' witty tricks make the story enjoyable despite its serious theme.
