Flamingo (Prose) – "The Rattrap" by Selma Lagerlöf

SHORT SUMMARY

"The Rattrap" is a story that explores human kindness and redemption. It follows a poor vagabond who survives by selling rattraps and stealing. He views the world as a giant rattrap that lures people with materialistic temptations. One day, he is taken in by a kind ironmaster and his daughter, Edla. Their generosity transforms him, making him a better person. The story conveys the power of love and compassion in changing a person’s life.

Characters

The Rattrap Seller

A poor vagabond who steals to survive but later reforms due to kindness shown by Edla.

Edla Willmansson

The ironmaster's daughter who treats the rattrap seller with compassion and helps him reform.

The Ironmaster

Owner of the ironworks who mistakes the peddler for an old friend and invites him home.

Theme

Compassion and Redemption

Edla’s kindness helps the peddler transform and leave his dishonest ways.

Materialism as a Trap

The world is compared to a rattrap that lures people with wealth and possessions.

Human Dignity

The story emphasizes treating all individuals with respect and kindness.

Important Questions

Why does the peddler compare the world to a rattrap?

He believes that materialistic temptations trap people just like a rattrap catches rats.

How does Edla Willmansson change the peddler’s life?

Her kindness and trust make him realize his mistakes and choose an honest path.

Why does the peddler leave a gift for Edla?

He leaves a rattrap and a letter as a token of gratitude for her kindness.

Key Quotes

"The world had never been kind to him, so it gave him unwanted joy to think ill of it."

"A day may come when you want to get away from the rattrap."

"She was not one to chase away a human being in trouble."

Literary Devices

Symbolism

The rattrap symbolizes the traps of materialism and greed in life.

Irony

The peddler, who deceives others, ultimately gets mistaken for a nobleman.

Metaphor

The world is compared to a rattrap, luring people into its traps.
